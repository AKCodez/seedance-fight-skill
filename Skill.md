# Seedance 2.0 — Ultra-Realistic Fight Scene Prompt Generator

Generate tight, character-limited, ultra-realistic fight prompts for Seedance 2.0 on Higgsfield. No fantasy. No glowing effects. No shockwaves. Just brutal, grounded, cinematic violence that looks like it was filmed, not generated.

---

## Golden Rules (Learned from Real Iteration)

These rules come from extensive prompt testing and iteration on Seedance 2.0:

### 1. CHARACTER LIMIT IS KING

Seedance 2.0 has a hard character limit on prompts. Every word must earn its place.

**Target prompt length: 1500–1800 characters max.**

How to stay under:
- Cut adjective stacking. "Towering, massive, chiseled, jacked" → "jacked"
- One sentence per action beat. Not three.
- Settings in 2–3 sentences max. Not a paragraph.
- No redundant atmosphere descriptions. Say it once.
- Combine beats: "he ducks, grabs their shirt, slams the keyboard into their face" is one sentence covering three actions

**What gets cut first when over limit:**
1. Ambient atmosphere details (dust floating, wind blowing)
2. Redundant camera directions
3. Sound design notes (Seedance generates audio natively)
4. Repeated emphasis on lighting
5. Resolution/FPS specs (use platform defaults)

### 2. ULTRA-REALISM ONLY

Seedance 2.0 renders realistic physics beautifully. Lean into that. Never break it with fantasy.

**NEVER include:**
- Shockwaves from impacts
- Glowing trails from weapons
- Keycaps/objects exploding like confetti or fireworks
- Impact craters in concrete or sand
- Bodies flying 10+ feet from a hit
- Slow-motion rainbow/light effects
- Stone cracking from punching a wall
- Any anime/superhero physics

**ALWAYS include:**
- Realistic body reactions (head snapping, stumbling, dropping)
- Close-up impact details (skin rippling, teeth flying, lips splitting)
- Blood that behaves like blood (dripping, smearing, mixing with rain/sweat)
- Real weapon weight (a keyboard swings like a keyboard, not a magic hammer)
- Breathing, exhaustion, sweat
- Bodies hitting real surfaces realistically (sliding down walls, crumpling)

### 3. THE CLOSE-UP IMPACT FORMULA

This is what makes fight scenes feel real. Every prompt needs 2–3 close-up impact moments.

**The formula:**
"Close-up: [weapon] connects with [body part] in slow motion, [realistic physical reaction], [specific consequence like a tooth flying out or lip splitting], [where the debris/tooth lands]."

**Examples that work:**
- "Close-up: keyboard connects with their cheek, skin rippling from impact, a tooth launches out and pings off a windshield"
- "Close-up: lips split on the keys, head whips sideways, a molar flies out and bounces off the concrete"
- "Close-up: nose crunches against the keyboard edge, blood on his knuckles, their legs give out"
- "Close-up: jaw shifts on impact, tooth pops out and clicks across the tile floor"

**Key detail — always say where the tooth/blood LANDS.** It grounds it in the environment:
- Tooth pings off a windshield
- Tooth bounces off a tire rim
- Tooth clicks across concrete
- Tooth lands in a puddle
- Tooth skips across sand
- Blood smears on brick
- Blood mixes with rain on their face

### 4. THE EMOTIONAL MID-FIGHT PAUSE

Every viral fight scene has a moment where the action stops and the character says something real. This is the structure:

**Setup:** Fighter stops. Breathing hard. Blood on hands. Leans on something or stands still.

**Environment responds:** Brief silence. Wind, rain, ambient sound only. One small environmental detail (a sign tumbling, a light flickering).

**The line:** Short. Vulnerable. Apologetic or raw honest. Under 10 words ideally.

**Lines that tested well:**
- "I'm sorry... I gotta eat too."
- "I'm sorry... you think I wanted this?"
- "I'm sorry... but I do this for us."
- "I'm sorry... I didn't have a choice."

**What makes a line work:**
- Starts with "I'm sorry" — vulnerability from someone who was just violent
- Second half is honest about money/survival — relatable
- Short enough to read on screen in 2 seconds
- Would work as a screenshot/quote in comments

**What doesn't work:**
- Long sentences (breaks the moment)
- Angry or aggressive tone (kills the vulnerability)
- Explaining or justifying (too wordy)
- Anything that sounds scripted or corporate

### 5. WAVE ATTACK STRUCTURE

One-vs-many fights need clear sequencing. Don't describe a mob attacking simultaneously — Seedance renders it as mush.

**Structure:**
1. First attacker charges alone → gets dealt with (include close-up)
2. Two more rush together → dealt with using environment
3. Pause → the emotional line
4. Final wave → montage of hits, end with final frame

**Each individual fight beat = one sentence:**
- "First one charges — he cracks the keyboard across their jaw"
- "Second rushes — he grabs their shirt, pulls them in, drives the keyboard into their face"

Don't over-choreograph. Seedance fills in the gaps. One action per attacker is enough.

### 6. SETTINGS THAT WORK

Based on testing, these settings render best and create natural fight geography:

**Tight/enclosed (forces close combat):**
- Underground parking garage — fluorescent lights, concrete pillars, cars to slam people onto
- Alley — brick walls, dumpsters, tight space
- Laundromat — machines, tight rows
- Subway platform — tiles, harsh light, echoing

**Open/dramatic (epic framing):**
- Rooftop at sunset/sunrise — city skyline, wind, golden light
- Colosseum/arena — sand, stone walls, sunset through archways
- Empty basketball court — chain fence, orange sky
- Construction site — exposed concrete, rebar, dust

**Atmospheric:**
- Rainy anything — puddles for reflections, wet skin, teeth landing in water
- Night + single light source — gas station, parking garage, streetlight under bridge

**Setting description budget: 2–3 sentences max.** Name the location, the lighting, one texture detail. Done.

### 7. WEAPON HANDLING

The weapon should feel like a real object with real weight.

**Keyboard as weapon (most tested):**
- Grip it by the cable for range (flail/whip style)
- Grip the body for close hits (blunt weapon)
- Keys pop off on impact (realistic — they do this)
- Gets progressively damaged through the fight (missing keys, cracked, smeared)
- Reference as "mechanical keyboard" not "RGB keyboard" for realism

**Other improvised weapons that work:**
- Selfie stick as a bo staff
- Ring light as a shield
- Laptop as a blunt weapon
- Phone as a thrown projectile
- Anything from the environment (car mirror, sign post)

**Weapon rule: mention the weapon's condition in the final frame.** "Keyboard missing keys and smeared red" sells the aftermath.

## Master Template

Use this structure for every prompt. Fill each section. Stay under 1800 characters total.

```
[SETTING — 2-3 sentences max]
Location. Lighting. One texture/atmosphere detail.

[CHARACTER — 2-3 sentences max]
Physical description. What they're wearing. Weapon in hand. Emotional state.

[THE MOB — 1-2 sentences]
Who they are. What they're carrying. Their energy.

[FIGHT SEQUENCE — bulk of the prompt]
Beat 1: First attacker → single action → close-up impact with tooth/blood detail
Beat 2: Second/third attackers → use environment (slam onto car, into wall, etc.)
Beat 3: More attackers → escalate (dual attacks, weapon by cable, etc.)

[THE PAUSE — 2-3 sentences]
He stops. Physical state (breathing, blood, leaning). Says the line.

[FINAL WAVE — 2-3 sentences]
They charge again. Montage of hits. Close-up of impacts.

[FINAL FRAME — 1-2 sentences]
Standing alone. Weapon condition. Environment aftermath. Breathing.
```

## Common Mistakes From Testing

**Mistake: Over-describing the setting**
Three sentences about dust particles, wind direction, and light color when one sentence does the job.
Fix: "[Location]. [Lighting]. [One detail]." Done.

**Mistake: Fantasy physics sneaking in**
"Shockwave ripples outward" or "body launches 15 feet" or "impact crater in the concrete."
Fix: People stumble, drop, slide, crumple. They don't fly.

**Mistake: Forgetting close-up impacts**
Writing "he hits them with the keyboard" without the slow-mo detail.
Fix: Every 2nd or 3rd hit gets a close-up with the tooth/blood formula.

**Mistake: The emotional line being too long**
"I'm sorry but you have to understand that I need the sponsorship money to continue creating content for all of you" — way too long.
Fix: Under 10 words. "I'm sorry... I gotta eat too."

**Mistake: Describing all attackers simultaneously**
"The mob rushes him and he fights them all" — Seedance can't render this clearly.
Fix: Sequential beats. One or two attackers at a time. Clear cause and effect.

**Mistake: Forgetting weapon degradation**
The keyboard looks pristine at the end of a brutal fight.
Fix: Final frame mentions missing keys, blood smears, cracks, damage.

**Mistake: No final frame**
The prompt just ends with the last hit.
Fix: Always end with a still moment — character alone, weapon hanging, aftermath visible, breathing.

**Mistake: Specifying RGB/glowing keyboard**
Saying "RGB keyboard with glowing keys" pushes Seedance toward fantasy rendering.
Fix: Say "mechanical keyboard" — the keys can have subtle color but don't emphasize glow.

## Line Generator Framework

For the mid-fight emotional pause, use this formula:

**"I'm sorry... [survival truth under 6 words]"**

Examples:
- "I'm sorry... I gotta eat too."
- "I'm sorry... this is how I survive."
- "I'm sorry... I didn't have a choice."
- "I'm sorry... you think I wanted this?"
- "I'm sorry... but I do this for us."
- "I'm sorry... I gotta live too man."

The "I'm sorry" opener is optional but tested strongest. The second half must be:
- About survival/money/necessity (not about being right)
- Conversational (not formal)
- Something the audience would screenshot
